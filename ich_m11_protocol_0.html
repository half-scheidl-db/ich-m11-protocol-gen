This fictional interventional clinical trial protocol draft is prepared in an ICH M11-style structure for a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase II trial in participants with moderate plaque psoriasis.

Short Title: DMR-201 in Moderate Plaque Psoriasis. Trial Acronym: CLEAR-SKIN.

Sponsor Name: Northlake Therapeutics Development, Ltd. This sponsor name is fictional and used solely for this draft protocol.

2 Protocol Synopsis

This is a fictional Phase II interventional trial designed to assess the efficacy, safety, pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics of DMR-201 compared with placebo in adult participants with moderate plaque psoriasis. Approximately 180 participants will be randomized in a 1:1:1 ratio to DMR-201 low dose, DMR-201 high dose, or placebo for 16 weeks of double-blind treatment, followed by a 12-week safety follow-up. The primary objective is to evaluate the effect of DMR-201 on psoriasis severity as measured by the proportion of participants achieving a 75% improvement from baseline in Psoriasis Area and Severity Index (PASI 75) at Week 16. Key secondary objectives include assessment of static Physician's Global Assessment response, change in body surface area involvement, participant-reported symptoms, safety, and pharmacokinetics.

2.1 Trial Design

Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group, multicenter Phase II trial.

2.2 Investigational Treatment

DMR-201 oral tablets administered once daily at 50 mg or 100 mg, or matching placebo, for 16 weeks.

2.3 Primary Endpoint

Proportion of participants achieving PASI 75 at Week 16.

2.4 Sample Size

Approximately 180 participants.

3 Schedule of Activities

Participants will attend a screening visit within 28 days before randomization, a baseline/randomization visit on Day 1, on-treatment visits at Weeks 2, 4, 8, 12, and 16, and follow-up visits at Weeks 20 and 28. Assessments include eligibility review, physical examination, vital signs, electrocardiogram, laboratory testing, psoriasis efficacy assessments, adverse event monitoring, concomitant medication review, and pharmacokinetic sampling at prespecified visits.

3.1 Screening Period

Informed consent, eligibility assessments, demographics, medical history, prior and concomitant medications, physical examination, vital signs, ECG, clinical laboratory tests, pregnancy testing where applicable, and baseline psoriasis assessments.

3.2 Treatment Period

Randomization at Day 1 followed by blinded treatment through Week 16. Efficacy, safety, and PK/PD assessments will be performed according to the schedule of activities.

3.3 Follow-Up Period

Safety follow-up through Week 28, including adverse event assessment, selected laboratory testing, and final trial completion procedures.

4 Introduction

Plaque psoriasis is a chronic inflammatory skin disorder characterized by erythematous, scaly plaques and variable symptom burden. Existing therapies may be limited by incomplete response, tolerability considerations, or route of administration. DMR-201 is a fictional orally administered small molecule with preclinical and early clinical findings suggesting modulation of inflammatory signaling pathways relevant to psoriasis pathophysiology. This protocol describes a Phase II trial intended to characterize the efficacy, safety, pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics of DMR-201 in adult participants with moderate plaque psoriasis.

4.1 Background

Psoriasis involves dysregulated immune pathways and keratinocyte hyperproliferation. Moderate disease may impair quality of life and require systemic treatment in selected participants.

4.2 Benefit-Risk Assessment

Potential benefits include reduction in psoriasis severity. Potential risks include adverse reactions associated with investigational treatment, including possible gastrointestinal, hepatic, dermatologic, or laboratory abnormalities. Ongoing safety monitoring and predefined stopping rules are included to manage risk.

5 Trial Objectives and Endpoints

The trial objectives and endpoints are defined to assess efficacy, safety, PK, and PD of DMR-201 in the target population.

5.1 Primary Objective and Endpoint

Objective: To evaluate the efficacy of DMR-201 compared with placebo in achieving clinically meaningful improvement in plaque psoriasis at Week 16. Endpoint: Proportion of participants achieving PASI 75 at Week 16.

5.2 Secondary Objectives and Endpoints

Secondary endpoints include proportion of participants achieving static Physician's Global Assessment score of 0 or 1 with at least a 2-point improvement from baseline at Week 16; change from baseline in PASI score at each post-baseline visit; change from baseline in body surface area affected; change from baseline in participant-reported itch numeric rating scale; incidence of treatment-emergent adverse events; changes in laboratory parameters, ECGs, and vital signs; and PK parameters including trough plasma concentrations.

5.3 Exploratory Objectives and Endpoints

Exploratory endpoints include biomarker changes in serum inflammatory mediators and optional skin biopsy transcriptomic markers, where consent is provided.

6 Trial Design

This is a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group Phase II trial. Eligible participants will be randomized 1:1:1 to receive DMR-201 50 mg, DMR-201 100 mg, or placebo once daily for 16 weeks. Randomization will be stratified by baseline PASI score category (<15 versus >=15) and prior systemic therapy use (yes versus no). The trial includes screening, treatment, and follow-up periods.

6.1 Scientific Rationale for Trial Design

A placebo-controlled design is appropriate to characterize dose response and estimate treatment effect in a mid-stage development setting. Parallel-group assignment reduces carryover effects and supports interpretable efficacy and safety comparisons.

6.2 Justification for Dose

Dose levels were selected based on fictional nonclinical and early clinical tolerability, PK, and target engagement findings. The selected doses are intended to provide exposure differentiation for dose-response assessment.

6.3 End of Trial Definition

The end of trial is defined as the date of the last participant's last visit or the date of trial termination, whichever occurs first.

7 Trial Population

The trial population will include adult participants with moderate plaque psoriasis meeting all eligibility criteria.

7.1 Inclusion Criteria

Participants must meet all of the following criteria: 1) age 18 to 75 years inclusive; 2) diagnosis of chronic plaque psoriasis for at least 6 months before screening; 3) moderate disease defined by PASI >=10, body surface area involvement >=10%, and static Physician's Global Assessment >=3 at screening and baseline; 4) candidate for systemic therapy or phototherapy; 5) willingness to comply with trial procedures; 6) provision of signed informed consent.

7.2 Exclusion Criteria

Participants meeting any of the following criteria will be excluded: 1) non-plaque forms of psoriasis as predominant disease; 2) active or latent infection requiring exclusion per protocol-defined criteria; 3) clinically significant hepatic, renal, cardiovascular, neurologic, hematologic, or psychiatric condition that may interfere with participation; 4) recent use of prohibited psoriasis therapies within protocol-defined washout periods; 5) history of malignancy within 5 years except adequately treated non-melanoma skin cancer or cervical carcinoma in situ; 6) pregnancy or breastfeeding; 7) known hypersensitivity to investigational product components.

7.3 Lifestyle Considerations

Participants will be instructed to avoid initiation of new topical or systemic psoriasis treatments, excessive ultraviolet exposure, and blood donation during participation and for a protocol-defined period after the last dose.

7.4 Screen Failures

Participants who do not meet eligibility criteria may be rescreened once if the reason for screen failure is temporary and resolution is documented.

8 Treatments

Investigational treatment, dosing, administration, packaging, accountability, and permitted concomitant therapy requirements are described in this section.

8.1 Investigational Treatment Description

DMR-201 is a fictional oral tablet formulation supplied as 50 mg strength tablets. Matching placebo tablets will be identical in appearance.

8.2 Dosage and Administration

Participants assigned to the low-dose arm will receive DMR-201 50 mg once daily. Participants assigned to the high-dose arm will receive DMR-201 100 mg once daily as two 50 mg tablets. Participants assigned to placebo will receive matching placebo tablets once daily. Treatment duration is 16 weeks.

8.3 Dose Modification

Temporary interruption is permitted for protocol-specified safety findings. Permanent discontinuation criteria include severe or recurrent clinically significant adverse reactions, specified liver chemistry elevations, pregnancy, or investigator determination of unacceptable risk.

8.4 Concomitant Therapy

Use of emollients is permitted. Low-potency topical corticosteroids for limited rescue use on non-target areas may be permitted after Week 8 under protocol-defined restrictions. Other systemic psoriasis treatments, biologics, phototherapy, and investigational agents are prohibited.

8.5 Treatment Compliance

Compliance will be assessed by tablet counts, participant diary review, and site interview. Participants with significant noncompliance may be counseled or discontinued at investigator discretion.

Participants may discontinue trial treatment due to adverse events, withdrawal of consent for treatment, pregnancy, protocol deviation affecting safety, investigator decision, sponsor decision, or trial termination.

9.2 Participant Withdrawal from the Trial

Participants may withdraw consent from the trial at any time. Reasonable efforts should be made to perform early termination assessments if consent allows.

9.3 Lost to Follow-Up

Sites must make documented attempts to contact participants who miss visits, using protocol-approved methods, before classifying them as lost to follow-up.

10 Trial Assessments and Procedures

Efficacy, safety, PK, PD, and other trial procedures will be conducted according to the schedule of activities and standardized assessment methods.

10.1 Efficacy Assessments

Efficacy assessments include PASI, static Physician's Global Assessment, body surface area involvement, target lesion photography where locally permitted, and participant-reported itch numeric rating scale.

10.2 Safety Assessments

Safety assessments include adverse events, serious adverse events, physical examinations, vital signs, ECGs, clinical laboratory testing including hematology, chemistry, liver function, renal function, urinalysis, and pregnancy testing where applicable.

10.3 Pharmacokinetic Assessments

Sparse plasma PK samples will be collected predose at selected visits and at specified postdose time points in a subset of participants.

10.4 Pharmacodynamic and Biomarker Assessments

Blood samples for inflammatory biomarkers will be collected at baseline and selected post-baseline visits. Optional skin biopsies may be collected at baseline and Week 16 for biomarker analysis.

11 Adverse Event Reporting

Adverse events will be collected from the time of informed consent until the end of safety follow-up. Serious adverse events must be reported by the investigator to the sponsor within protocol-defined timelines. Severity will be graded according to a prespecified toxicity grading scale, and causality will be assessed by the investigator.

11.1 Definition of Adverse Events

An adverse event is any untoward medical occurrence in a participant administered a trial intervention and does not necessarily have a causal relationship with treatment.

11.2 Serious Adverse Events

A serious adverse event is any event that results in death, is life-threatening, requires inpatient hospitalization or prolongation of existing hospitalization, results in persistent or significant disability or incapacity, results in a congenital anomaly or birth defect, or is an important medical event.

11.3 Pregnancy Reporting

Pregnancy occurring during the trial must be reported promptly and followed to outcome in accordance with protocol procedures and applicable regulations.

12 Statistics

Statistical methods are planned to characterize efficacy and safety in the randomized population.

12.1 Sample Size Determination

A sample size of approximately 180 participants, with 60 participants per arm, is intended to provide adequate precision for estimation of treatment effects and support dose-response evaluation in this Phase II setting. Formal power assumptions are based on fictional planning parameters.

12.2 Analysis Sets

The randomized set will include all randomized participants. The intent-to-treat set will include all randomized participants analyzed according to assigned treatment. The safety set will include all participants receiving at least one dose of trial treatment.

12.3 Primary Analysis

The primary endpoint will be analyzed using a stratified Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test comparing each DMR-201 dose group with placebo, with stratification factors as randomized. Missing Week 16 response data will be handled using nonresponder imputation.

12.4 Secondary Analyses

Continuous endpoints will be analyzed using mixed models for repeated measures or analysis of covariance, as appropriate. Multiplicity control will follow a prespecified hierarchical testing procedure.

12.5 Interim Analysis

No formal interim efficacy analysis is planned. Periodic blinded safety review may be conducted by a data monitoring committee or equivalent safety review body.
